Programs that help pay for training

Most Hamilton County residents qualify for at least one of these. The school's financial aid office or your local WorkOne can confirm eligibility.

  • WIOA (Workforce Innovation & Opportunity Act) — federal training dollars routed through Indiana DWD. Often covers full tuition for in-demand training. Apply through your local WorkOne: 1-800-891-6499.
  • Workforce Ready Grant — Indiana program covering tuition for high-demand short-credential certificates at Ivy Tech, Vincennes, and others. Indiana CHE info →
  • Next Level Jobs Employer Training Grant — for people whose employer enrolls them in upskilling. 1-800-891-6499.
  • Pell Grant — federal grant for low- to moderate-income students. Doesn't have to be paid back. File the FAFSA at studentaid.gov.
  • Apprenticeship — earn while you learn. Most Registered Apprenticeships pay $18–$30/hour during training. Browse apprenticeships in Hamilton County →
  • Employer tuition reimbursement — many local employers (Lilly, Riverview Health, IU Health, Kroger, others) pay all or part of training costs. Ask HR.
  • Hamilton County Community Foundation scholarships — local funds for trades and credentials. 317-843-2483. hamiltoncountycommunityfoundation.org

Walk in or call. Their workforce navigators are good at matching people to the right credential. 317-296-5400. ivytech.edu/hamilton-county →
